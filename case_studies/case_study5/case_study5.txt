CASE STUDY 05
SampleBuilding.ifc
Revit 2024 · IFC4 · Multi-storey commercial building — concrete shear wall core with steel perimeter frame

THE PROBLEM
This is a production-scale commercial building model.
1,221 structural elements processed:
Concrete shear wall core for lateral resistance
Steel perimeter gravity frame
Composite metal deck floors
Deep foundation system
At this scale, errors do not appear as obvious anomalies. They are embedded in classification, layering, and incomplete geometric representation.
The question is not whether errors exist.
The question is whether the extraction system detects them before they reach the BOQ.
Four findings. Each with direct cost impact.

FINDING 1 — FOUNDATION SYSTEM UNDERCOUNTED BY A FACTOR OF 6.4
A class-only IFC extraction reads IfcFooting and returns 9 elements and 56.88 m³. This is incomplete.
The model contains an additional foundation system encoded as IfcSlab with PredefinedType BASESLAB.
SFD_Round drilled caissons — 30" and 24" diameter — 65 elements
SFD_Pile_Cap rectangular caps — 13 elements
FNDN-36 mat foundations — 4 elements
Strip footings — 9 elements
Total — 90 foundation elements. 361.62 m³.
A class-only workflow excludes the majority of the foundation system. 304.74 m³ missed. On a real project that is not a rounding error. That is a full foundation system absent from procurement quantities.
The correct signal is not class. It is PredefinedType.

FINDING 2 — 51% OF A SHEAR WALL'S VOLUME DOES NOT EXIST
Three shear walls contain major openings.
Wall A — 51% of gross volume removed by openings
Wall B — 47% removed
Wall C — 26% removed
GrossVolume is the solid wall before openings. NetVolume is the geometry after openings are subtracted. These are materially different quantities.
In the most extreme case, more than half of the reported wall volume is void.
Shear wall system — 58 walls. 1,164.94 m³ total. Using GrossVolume would systematically overstate concrete across every perforated wall in the lateral core.
NetVolume used consistently and verified against Bonsai for all 58 elements.
The distinction is not computational. It is commercial.

FINDING 3 — €14,400 OF PHANTOM CONCRETE COST IN 12 COMPOSITE SLABS
12 floor slabs are NW Concrete on Metal Deck — Vulcraft 2VLI system. Total IFC volume — 601.05 m³.
At €120 per m³, rib displacement from the Vulcraft 2VLI deck profile overstates concrete by an estimated €10,800 to €14,400 on this element group alone.
The cause — the 2" steel ribs displace concrete but the IFC geometry models these slabs as flat plates. Rib geometry was never modeled. The IFC volume includes the full depth as solid concrete.
The engine correctly isolates the concrete layer and excludes the steel decking from quantities. But it cannot correct what the geometry does not model.
This limitation is documented in the validation report with the correction range. The QS applies the rib displacement factor from structural drawings. The IFC volume is the starting point — not the final answer.

FINDING 4 — STRUCTURAL AND NON-STRUCTURAL ELEMENTS AT THE CONCRETE-TO-STEEL TRANSITION
At the elevator pit transition zone — 50 IfcBuildingElementProxy elements identified. Steel embed plates and headed anchor studs — SCN_Embed and SCN_HAS systems connecting the concrete core to the steel frame above.
These are physical components. They are not structural concrete elements for BOQ purposes.
Correctly excluded from concrete quantities. Zero volume contribution. No impact on structural totals.
The concrete-to-steel interface is one of the most failure-prone classification zones in mixed-material buildings. No misclassification observed.

VALIDATION PIPELINE
BASESLAB detection across foundation subtypes — caissons, pile caps, mat foundations

Material and section-based steel exclusion — columns, beams, trusses

ONE HONEST LIMITATION
Steel reinforcement is not explicitly modeled. Total reinforcement — 221,040 kg — ratio estimated from BS EN 1992 and ACI 318. Uncertainty range ±30 to 50%. Not suitable for procurement or fabrication.
Composite slab rib displacement — 90 to 120 m³ — is not resolved in IFC geometry and requires QS-level correction from structural drawings.

WHY THIS MATTERS
This is not an unusual building. It is a standard commercial typology.
But at this scale — entire foundation systems are missed under class-only extraction. Shear wall volumes include physically removed geometry. Composite slabs embed unmodeled displacement errors. Steel hardware exists inside structural zones and must be excluded.
IFC classes alone are insufficient for system-level interpretation.
None of these trigger failures in conventional workflows. They produce valid-looking quantities that silently diverge from construction reality.
At production scale, IFC validation is not about detecting errors. It is about correctly interpreting every quantity before it becomes a cost decision.
